Is your business ready for the incoming protect duty legislation?

Since 2017, terror attacks in the UK have taken place leaving 36 people dead, around 390 physically injured and many more emotionally traumatised. These tragic attacks caused needless deaths and casualties among innocent people going about their everyday lives.

There is currently no legislative requirement for businesses to employ specific terrorist-related security measures in the vast majority of public places.

However, the UK Home Office has pledged to introduce new legislation called Protect Duty . This means that the people in charge need to consider specifically the risk of terrorist attacks and take proportionate and reasonable measures to prepare and protect staff and the wider public.

Easily and cost-effectively manage this complex new legislation.

Protect Duty, otherwise known as Martyn’s Law (in honour of Martyn Hett – one of the 22 victims of the 2017 Manchester Arena Bombings), will be a new piece of anti-terrorism legislation that Prime Minister, Rishi Sunack has stated the first draft legislation will be ready by Spring 2023.

Hundreds of thousands of businesses and venues across the UK will be legally required to comply.
